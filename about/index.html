About Me

My journey so far has been shaped by curiosity, discipline, and a very clear desire to build something meaningful—for my family and for myself.

I completed my B.Com (Honours) in 2024, but my interest never stayed confined to textbooks or traditional career paths. During my studies, a case study on Manyavar introduced me to how simple data science tools were used to scale a legacy business. That insight changed the way I looked at data. I realized that behind strong decisions and sustainable growth, there is often a layer of machine learning quietly doing the heavy lifting.

Coming from a commerce background, stepping into machine learning wasn’t comfortable—or obvious. But I’ve never been afraid of learning from scratch. After graduation, I deliberately built my foundation in mathematics, statistics, and algorithms, and in 2025, I formally pursued a Diploma in Data Science at IT-Vedant to give structure to my transition.

Today, I consider myself a machine learning enthusiast at an early-career stage, actively learning by building. I enjoy working across the full data lifecycle—cleaning messy data, exploring patterns, engineering features, training models, and evaluating them in ways that actually make sense for real-world use. I’ve worked on datasets across food-tech, healthcare, and finance, and built practical projects like recommendation systems, classifiers, and emotion detection models using Python, SQL, Excel, Power BI, and ML frameworks.

Interestingly, Python has been part of my life much longer than my formal ML journey. I started using it back in 2018, initially as a creative tool—building apps with Kivy/KivyMD, creating Telegram bots, automating daily tasks, and experimenting with web scraping. That early experimentation shaped how I approach machine learning today: practical first, theory where it matters, and always focused on solving real problems.

Outside of tech, I value self-discipline and self-expression just as much. I train in calisthenics, learning through experimentation and consistency rather than shortcuts. I also enjoy sketching, and I run a sketching page called “sptrop”, where drawing helps me slow down, observe, and think visually—skills that surprisingly carry over into how I approach data and models.

At the core of everything I do is a clear personal goal: to build a life that allows me to give my family comfort, security, and freedom, while also pursuing my own passions. One of those dreams is owning an ADV motorcycle—not just to ride, but to truly understand. I want to be capable enough to repair, maintain, and trust my machine, travel across India first, and then explore other countries.

For me, that mindset—understanding systems deeply instead of just using them—applies equally to bikes, bodies, art, and machine learning.

Right now, I’m focused on growing as a machine learning practitioner—learning continuously, building consistently, and aiming for roles where ML is applied thoughtfully, not blindly. I’m not chasing shortcuts. I’m building capability, one layer at a time.
