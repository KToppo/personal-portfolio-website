PCOD & PCOS Health Data Analysis

Exploring patterns responsibly — without crossing into diagnosis or medical claims.

This project was created to explore how data analysis can be used to understand complex health conditions without crossing into unsafe or misleading conclusions.

PCOD and PCOS are widely discussed but often poorly understood, especially when conversations rely on anecdotes instead of data. My goal was not to “predict disease”, but to analyze patterns, correlations, and lifestyle indicators present in real-world health data.

I approached this project with one clear boundary: analysis for awareness and insight, not medical judgment.

Exploratory visual analysis of health indicators related to PCOD and PCOS.

How I Approached the Problem

The dataset contained multiple physiological and lifestyle-related attributes associated with women’s health. Instead of treating it as a classification task, I focused on Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) to answer practical, responsible questions such as:

  • How do health indicators vary between affected and non-affected groups?
  • Are certain patterns consistently associated with PCOD/PCOS?
  • Which features show stronger relationships, and which are mostly noise?

I began with careful data cleaning and validation, handling missing values, inconsistencies, and outliers — an especially important step for health-related data where errors can mislead interpretation.

From there, I used visualizations and statistical summaries to explore:

  • Hormonal and metabolic indicators
  • Body metrics and lifestyle variables
  • Distribution differences across groups
  • Correlations that may warrant deeper clinical investigation

At every step, the focus remained on interpreting trends responsibly, not forcing conclusions.

Key Insights and Observations

The analysis revealed that:

  • Certain health indicators show noticeable distribution differences between groups
  • Some commonly assumed factors have weaker relationships than expected
  • A small subset of features consistently stands out across multiple views of the data

More importantly, the project highlighted how easily misleading conclusions can emerge if analysis is not done carefully — reinforcing why EDA is critical before any modeling or inference.

What This Project Actually Demonstrates

  • Responsible handling of sensitive health data
  • Strong EDA skills applied to a real-world, high-impact domain
  • The ability to translate numerical patterns into human-understandable insights
  • Awareness of ethical boundaries in data science

It reflects a mindset that prioritizes interpretation, caution, and clarity over flashy claims.

Why This Project Matters in My Portfolio

Unlike recommendation systems or classifiers, this project required:

  • Slower, more deliberate analysis
  • Ethical awareness
  • Clear communication without overclaiming

It strengthened my ability to work with real-world data where mistakes matter, and where insight is more valuable than prediction.

Health data is powerful — and dangerous if misused. This project respects that reality. It focuses on understanding patterns, raising the right questions, and demonstrating analytical discipline — not pretending to replace medical expertise.
